About Me

I'm a Mathematics PhD Student at Northwestern University working under the supervision of Steve Zelditch. Broadly speaking my interests lie in partial differential equations related to quantum mechanics. Recently I have also taken an interest in deep learning and its applications to audio synthesis.

Research

My research lies in the intersection of microlocal analysis, complex analysis, and geometry. I study the asymptotic behavior of complexified eigenfunctions on Riemannian manifolds. These may be thought of as the curved space analogue of Husimi Distributions which are commonly used in the phase-space formulation of quantum mechanics.
